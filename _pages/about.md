I am a professor of management in the Faculty of Management, Economics, and Social Sciences at the [University of Cologne](https://wiso.uni-koeln.de/en/) in the [Corporate Development](https://cd.wiso.uni-koeln.de/en/) department. I am also senior research fellow at the [Max Planck Institute for Research on Collective Goods](https://www.coll.mpg.de/), a research fellow at [IZA](https://www.iza.org/person/790/dirk-sliwka) and [CESifo](https://www.cesifo.org/en), and a member of [C-SEB](https://c-seb.de/) and [ECONtribute](https://econtribute.de/).

My research focuses on designing effective Human Resource Management practices within firms and evaluating their impact on behavior, performance, and well-being. I am particularly interested in the design of incentives and performance management processes as well as the role of social norms in organizations. 

My main research fields of interest are:
 - Organizational/Personnel Economics
 - Human Resource Management
 - Evaluation of Management Practices in Field Experiments 
 - Behavioral Economics
 - Management Accounting
